<BodyText>
**Common entry points:**

- **Security Analyst**: Monitor systems, investigate alerts, respond to incidents. Entry-level role that builds broad understanding.
- **Security Engineer**: Implement and maintain security tools, configure controls, automate tasks. Technical focus.
- **GRC Analyst**: Governance, risk, and compliance. Policy writing, audit support, risk assessment. Less technical, more process-focused.

**Typical progression:**
Junior Analyst â†’ Senior Analyst â†’ Lead/Architect or Manager â†’ Director â†’ CISO

**Alternative paths:**
- Start in IT/networking and transition to security
- Move from development to application security
- Shift from auditing to GRC leadership
</BodyText>

<Callout title="Reality check" type="info">
Most cybersecurity roles are not like movies. You will spend time on documentation, meetings, risk assessments, and process improvement. The "hacking" stereotype represents a tiny fraction of actual security work.
</Callout>
