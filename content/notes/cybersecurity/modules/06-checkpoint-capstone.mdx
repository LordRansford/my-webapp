<SubsectionHeader emoji="üíº" id="practical-exam">
  Practical Exam
</SubsectionHeader>

<BodyText>
The practical exam consists of three hands-on tasks that apply your Foundations knowledge. You have 90 minutes (suggested) to complete all tasks. This is an open-book assessment.

**Task 1: Phishing Email Analysis (30 points)**

You will analyze 3 email examples (provided separately) and for each:
- Classify as phishing or legitimate
- Identify specific indicators (sender domain, urgency tactics, link analysis)
- Explain the attack vector and potential impact
- Recommend response actions

**Task 2: Attack Surface Diagram (35 points)**

Given a scenario (small business with website, email, file sharing, and remote workers), you will:
- Create a visual diagram showing all components
- Identify and mark entry points
- Draw trust boundaries
- Highlight data flows
- List 5 key vulnerabilities with risk ratings

**Task 3: Breach Incident Report (35 points)**

Read a provided breach scenario (200-300 words) and write a 2-page incident report covering:
- **What happened**: Clear timeline and summary
- **How it happened**: Attack vector analysis
- **What failed**: Identify 3+ failed security controls
- **Recommendations**: 3 specific, feasible preventive controls

**Deliverables:**
- Submit as single PDF document
- Include your name and completion date
- Label each task clearly
- Show your work and reasoning

**Grading Rubric**: [View detailed rubric ‚Üí](/docs/courses/rubrics/foundations-practical-exam)

**Passing Score**: 70/100 points

**Retake Policy**: If you score below 70%, you may retake once after reviewing feedback and relevant modules.
</BodyText>

<Callout title="Practical Exam Purpose" type="info">
The practical exam tests your ability to apply concepts, not just recall facts. In real cybersecurity work, you'll analyze threats, assess systems, and communicate findings. These tasks simulate activities you'll perform in security roles.

Take the exam seriously, but don't stress‚Äîit's designed to reinforce learning, not trick you.
</Callout>

<SubsectionHeader emoji="üìã" id="option-a">
  Option A: Personal Security Improvement Plan
</SubsectionHeader>

<BodyText>
Create and implement a comprehensive 20-point security improvement plan for your personal digital life.

**Deliverables:**
1. **Current State Assessment** (1-2 pages)
   - Audit of devices, accounts, and current practices
   - Honest assessment of vulnerabilities
   - Risk prioritization

2. **Improvement Checklist** (20 items minimum)
   - Specific, measurable actions
   - Prioritized by risk and feasibility
   - Timeline for implementation

3. **Implementation Evidence** (screenshots, descriptions)
   - Proof of completion for 18+ items
   - Before/after comparisons where relevant
   - Redact sensitive information

4. **Reflection Essay** (500 words)
   - Challenges encountered
   - Surprises or unexpected learnings
   - How habits have changed
   - Future security goals

**Example checklist items:**
- Enable 2FA on 10 critical accounts
- Migrate 50 passwords to password manager
- Delete 15 unused online accounts
- Review and update privacy settings on 5 services
- Set up automatic backups
- Enable full-disk encryption
- Configure firewall rules
- Audit app permissions on mobile devices

**Grading Rubric**: [View detailed rubric ‚Üí](/docs/courses/rubrics/foundations-capstone-projects#option-a)

**Success Indicator**: You've made measurable improvements to your security posture with evidence to prove it.
</BodyText>
