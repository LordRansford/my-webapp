# Digitalisation Foundations

<LevelProgressBar courseId="digitalisation" levelId="foundations" sectionIds={digitalisationSectionManifest.foundations} />

<CPDTracker courseId="digitalisation" levelId="foundations" estimatedHours={8} />

Digitalisation is not a tool rollout. It is the deliberate redesign of how value is created using data, platforms, and better ways of working. These foundations focus on outcomes, not buzzwords.

---

## Why digitalisation matters

<SectionProgressToggle courseId="digitalisation" levelId="foundations" sectionId="dig-foundations-context-and-drivers" />

Digitalisation matters because expectations are higher, services are more complex, and regulation is tighter. In energy, the pressure is even greater: net zero targets, real time grid data, and consumer trust all depend on digital capability.

<Callout variant="concept" type="info">
If core services cannot be delivered securely and simply through digital channels, you have a digitalisation problem.
</Callout>

Digitalisation is also about focus. It forces clarity on what value looks like and who the change is for. When leaders treat it as a technology project, it usually stalls.

## Data, standards and interoperability

<SectionProgressToggle courseId="digitalisation" levelId="foundations" sectionId="dig-foundations-data-and-standards" />

A <GlossaryTip term="dataset">A structured collection of related data used for analysis or operations.</GlossaryTip> is only useful when people trust it. A <GlossaryTip term="schema">The structure and meaning of fields in a dataset.</GlossaryTip> tells everyone what the data means. A <GlossaryTip term="data model">A shared representation of how data is organised and related.</GlossaryTip> keeps systems aligned.

An <GlossaryTip term="API">A defined way for systems to request and exchange data.</GlossaryTip> is the bridge. <GlossaryTip term="interoperability">The ability for different systems to exchange and use data consistently.</GlossaryTip> is the goal.

## Platforms, journeys and dashboards

<SectionProgressToggle courseId="digitalisation" levelId="foundations" sectionId="dig-foundations-platforms-and-journeys" />

A <GlossaryTip term="platform">A shared set of capabilities and services used across teams.</GlossaryTip> keeps digital work consistent. A <GlossaryTip term="journey">The sequence of steps a user takes to achieve a goal.</GlossaryTip> shows where data and service design must line up. A <GlossaryTip term="user story">A short description of what a user needs and why.</GlossaryTip> keeps teams focused on real outcomes.

Dashboards turn journeys into signals. They show where digital journeys break, where demand changes, and where teams need to respond.

## Risks, governance and people

<SectionProgressToggle courseId="digitalisation" levelId="foundations" sectionId="dig-foundations-risks-governance" />

Digitalisation creates new risks: data quality problems, weak ownership, and security gaps. Governance makes those risks visible and managed. People still do the work, so roles and responsibility must be clear.

<Callout variant="concept" type="info">
Digitalisation without governance is speed without control.
</Callout>
