## Strategy and target state architecture

<SectionProgressToggle courseId="digitalisation" levelId="advanced" sectionId="dig-advanced-strategy-target-state" />

A <GlossaryTip term="target state">The future view of how services, data, and operating models should work together.</GlossaryTip> keeps strategy concrete. A <GlossaryTip term="reference architecture">A shared blueprint that shows the major components and how they connect.</GlossaryTip> keeps teams aligned as they deliver in parallel.

The hardest part is focus. You cannot improve every capability at once, so you choose the few that unlock the rest.

<Callout variant="concept" type="info">
Strategy is a sequence of hard choices, not a list of everything you want.
</Callout>

## Data sharing, models and standards

<SectionProgressToggle courseId="digitalisation" levelId="advanced" sectionId="dig-advanced-data-sharing-and-standards" />

At scale, digitalisation depends on shared meaning. A <GlossaryTip term="canonical model">A common structure that keeps core data definitions consistent.</GlossaryTip> reduces translation work. A <GlossaryTip term="data sharing agreement">A documented agreement that covers access, usage, and accountability.</GlossaryTip> keeps trust intact.

Interoperability is not just a technical issue. It is legal, operational, and cultural. You need incentives for everyone to keep the model accurate over time.

## Platforms, ecosystems and governance

<SectionProgressToggle courseId="digitalisation" levelId="advanced" sectionId="dig-advanced-platforms-and-ecosystems" />

A <GlossaryTip term="platform">A shared set of services and data that multiple teams build on.</GlossaryTip> only works when governance is clear. An <GlossaryTip term="ecosystem">A network of organisations that exchange data and services.</GlossaryTip> needs balanced roles so trust stays high.

This is where stewardship matters. If one actor abuses the system, everyone pays the cost.

## Measurement, risk and roadmaps

<SectionProgressToggle courseId="digitalisation" levelId="advanced" sectionId="dig-advanced-measurement-risk-and-roadmaps" />

Digitalisation is never complete, so you need a way to steer. A <GlossaryTip term="risk appetite">The level of risk an organisation is willing to accept.</GlossaryTip> guides how fast you move. A <GlossaryTip term="roadmap">A staged plan that sequences change over time.</GlossaryTip> keeps teams aligned.

If you cannot measure adoption, quality, and stability together, you will eventually drift.
